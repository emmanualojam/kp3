About Us

Our Work

The Kwara State Public Private Partnership Bureau (KP3) is the Kwara State Governmentâ€™s (KWSG) one-stop shop for all internal and external parties who are interested in the non-traditional approaches to partnering for the delivery of critical assets and services, through mutually beneficial agreements that assure improved quality of life and economic rewards.



Our Purpose

  1. Develop and support delivery of an infrastructure strategy for the entire State.
  2. Ensure a stable pipeline of projects.
  3. Be the focal point for infrastructure investors and industry
  4. Tackle cross-cutting policy issues.
  5. Ensure value for money for KWSG investments
  6. Spearhead a comprehensive paradigm shift of public and private participation
  7. Negotiate on behalf of the KWSG and residents of Kwara
  8. Provide information and guidance in an advisory capacity.



Our Role

  1. Act as project advisor providing hands-on technical assistance from inception to completion
  2. Render support to MDAs by drawing upon best practices; and recommend MDAs’ projects for approval
  3. Ensure approvals are processed efficiently
  4. Ensure communication across stakeholders
  5. Act as a one-stop-shop for priv ate parties
  6. Manage project planning, procurement and implementation
  7. Direct and manage the work of transaction consultants and approve milestone payments
  8. Lead public communications and supervise the entire bidding process as it pertains to all PPP projects
  9. Assess fiscal impact and manage contingent liabilities, consequently recommending terms as presented or modifying them
  10. Internal review of the PPP process and projects approved on bi-annual basis, and as required by His Excellency



Our Performance Assessment Criteria

  1. Strategy – plan, develop and implement a strategy for KWSG infrastructure.
  2. Policy – lead on PPP and broader infrastructure delivery policy.
  3. Finance – assess options to attract additional capital to finance KWSG infrastructure.
  4. Prioritisation – advise MDAs on spending priorities.
  5. Support – MDAs on delivery of major projects/programmes.
  6. Scrutiny – support all assessments and processes.
  7. Market leadership – Government’s focal point for infrastructure investors, developers and operators.
  8. Capacity building – for all MDAs for PPP initiatives



Our Expected Outcomes

  1. Project identification and business case development with MDAs
  2. Design guidelines and criteria for transparent decision-making processes.
  3. Analysis of project’s fundamentals – Value for Money (VfM), value addition, etc.
  4. Supervision and review of the operational framework per project.
  5. Review of PPP options and recommended transaction structure.
  6. Review of financial modelling of the PPP project.
  7. Proposal development and/or amendment of procurement laws/principles.
  8. Proposal development and/or amendment of concession laws to attract private partners.
